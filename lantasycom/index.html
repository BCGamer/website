FAQs

Have some questions? Here’s some answers.

What is a LAN Party?

An event where people bring their own computers, and play competitive or casual video games on a LAN (local area network), as a collective. The event typically lasts the duration of a weekend, and involves many different gaming tournaments.

What should I bring?

• Identification (We need proof that you’re you!)
• Bring a Guardian or a signed parental consent waiver if you’re under 17
• Your Computer (and all applicable cabling)
• A Single Monitor (and all applicable cabling)
• A mouse or trackpad/ball
• Your mousepad (This is important!)
• Keyboard
• 30-foot LAN Cable – in excellent condition (Not Orange/Purple/Pink/Green colour!!). They will be available onsite for $20 each.
• Power cable / Other cabling – For your Monitor / Computer. Have a neat peripheral that needs a cable too? Bring that. Then double check it. And bring a spare.
• Powerbar – Bring one powerbar, preferrably with an 8ft cable. Do NOT bring a UPS for your computer to the LAN.
• PC Headset – Headphones WITH A MIC. Note: Desktop mics and speakers are not permitted in the LAN. Remember that LAN events are quite loud — a headset with excellent sound insulation will make a huge difference for you.
• Food & Beverage Money – There are nearby food trucks, restaurants and fast food establishments.

What should I do to get ready?

Everyone needs to take care of themselves first. Here are some tips that have worked for us:

Get a good night’s sleep the week leading up to the LAN. This is crucial. The LAN is open 17 hours a day so getting a good night’s sleep the week leading up to the LAN, will ensure you can game at your best.

Eat & Drink – Stay well-fed, stay hydrated to keep going at maximum performance. Remember no outside food is allowed into the event so plan on having meal breaks.

Coordinate seating with your friends – Want to sit with Johnny because he’s on your LOL team? Probably a good idea to arrive with him. Teams who arrive together WILL be given preferential seating.

Arrive early – If you want to get going right on time, arrive early. While we will be seating as many LANNERS as fast as we can, you don’t want to get stuck in the back of the line!

Come out with an open mind – There will be many things to do, games to play, and there is a possibility that there will be new games for you to experience as well as introducing people to some of your favourite games, meeting new people etc.
